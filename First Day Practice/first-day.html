Jonathan's "Clear" Dream Sandwich

Materials

  • Baguette or ciabatta bread or hero bread - or your favorite bread that’s fresh from the baker.
  • Arugula or baby spinach – whichever is fresh and in season.
  • Swiss Cheese
  • Comté Cheese – French if in stock or Swiss if you cannot find the French version.
  • Pastrami - preferably from Katz's Delicatessen or Zabar’s.
    • If from Zabar’s make sure its sliced paper thin.
  • White Onions
  • Mushrooms - Baby Bella.
  • Hungarian Hot Paprika
  • Black Pepper from a Peppercorn mill shaker thing
  • Fresh Garlic clove
  • Peter Luger Steak Sause and Grey Poupon Country Dijon or Katz’s Mustard
  • One knife for cutting
  • One butter knife for spreading
  • Unsalted Organic Butter
  • Pan
  • Toaster or Panini Press
  • Paper towels
  • Cutting Board
  • Plates and bowls

    • Bonus

  • Onion rings or waffle fries
  • Cape Cod waffle potato chips and or Cape Cod Barbeque potato chips.
  • Champagne, Beer, white wine, Stewart’s Cream soda, San Pellegrino Sparkling Natural water, or water.

Pre-Instruction Notes

For this sandwich, I will be using my preferred ingredients from above.

You can use the other options if not supplied in your area or change depending on your spice preference.

All procedures assume you have already arrived at home or place of cooking and un-wrapped all items from the stores as well as opened all items.

All procedures also assume the chef is fully set up to cook with sleeves rolled up a clean work station and washed hands.

Steps

  1. Take Arugula and clean them under water place them on paper towels.
    1. Flatten and dry.
      1. Optional - use a salad spinner.
    2. Cut the tips of the roots
  2. Place the Ciabatta bread on the cutting board.
  3. Take a knife (Bread Knife) and proceed to safely cut the bread Horizontally (the long way) making sure both halves are even.
  4. Place the bread from the cut board onto a plate open.
  5. Clean the crumbs from the cutting board.
  6. Take a butter knife and dip and spread Katz mustard on both open sides of bread continue until both sides evenly are evenly covered.
  7. Heat the turkey and Pastrami for about 30 seconds to minute on medium in a clean pan with Comté and swiss cheese.
  8. While cooking place the dried Arugula on the bottom half the open bread.
  9. Slide it out and place on top of the arugula.
  10. On the cutting board place one clove of garlic.
  11. Smash one clove of garlic and remove the skin/rapper leave the garlic on the board and toss the wrapper.
  12. Dice the garlic into fine pieces.
  13. Clean the mushrooms and onion under running water.
  14. Take cut an onion in half take the other half and store it. With the half still on the board dice it into fine cubes.
  15. Take three mushrooms that were cleaned and dice them into slices.
  16. In the same pan that you cooked the meat add one tablespoon(tbs) of butter (unsalted) or one-eighth stick of butter (unsalted) on medium heat and spread evenly until melted.
  17. Take the diced garlic and place in the pan with the diced onions.
  18. Place in the sliced mushrooms into the pan.
  19. While it is cooking put in about one quarter tablespoon Hungarian Hot paprika and two cracks (two twists) of fresh ground pepper from your pepper mill. Cook until lightly brown.
  20. Place on top of the pastrami, turkey, and cheese.
  21. Place some Peter Luger steak Sause on top of the mushroom garlic unions.
  22. Close the sandwich and toast in a panini press for one minute.
  23. Take sandwich out of a Panini press. Take a cooking or steak knife and cut in half vertically on an about 30° to 45° angle.

  24. Bonus - Pairs great with Champagne, Beer, white wine, Stewart’s Cream soda, San Pellegrino Sparkling Natural water, or ice water.
  25. Bonus - If you want to serve with onion rings or waffle fries or waffle potato chips.

Side Note:

  • When preping the smoked turkey roll it so it looks like a cylinder and keep repeating until it lines one to two layers or like a crumbled up ball or folded three to four times.
